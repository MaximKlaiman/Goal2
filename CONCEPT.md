# Product Concept — Full Brief

## Origin

The idea: an app that lets you photograph your syllabus and, like Runna for running, builds a study schedule to help you meet your academic goals.

The basic version already exists — Ahead, Syllabus AI, OVR IT, StudyFetch. They all do syllabus scanning and schedule generation. The market isn't missing a syllabus scanner. It's missing genuine coaching intelligence underneath one.

---

## What Runna Actually Does (And Why It Matters)

Runna doesn't just put runs on a calendar. It takes an audacious goal — run a sub-4-hour marathon — and reverse-engineers every single day from it. Each session has a specific purpose inside a periodized plan. The prescription is built on actual training science: base building, threshold work, taper, recovery.

The equivalent for studying would require:
- Spaced repetition logic
- Interleaving across subjects
- Retrieval practice over passive review
- Effort estimation correction
- Cognitive load awareness

No existing app does this. They divide time evenly across deadlines. That's not coaching — that's a to-do list with dates.

---

## The Four Pillars

### 1. Output
The tangible behavior that produces the goal — distinct from the outcome itself. You can't control whether you get an A, but you can control whether you complete three retrieval practice sessions on Chapter 4. Output is about owning the behavior, not the result.

### 2. Structure
The adaptive scaffolding around when and how work gets done. There are two layers:
- **Temporal** — when study sessions happen (most apps solve this)
- **Behavioral** — what actually happens inside those sessions (almost no apps solve this)

Structure has to account for the real shape of a student's week, not just deadline proximity.

### 3. Prioritization
Dynamically determining what matters most on a given day. Static prioritization breaks down immediately. Good prioritization is simultaneously:
- Deadline-driven
- Difficulty-weighted
- Capacity-aware

These three factors are often in tension. The system has to resolve that tension automatically.

### 4. Intelligence
The system that serves the other three. Intentionally last — because the AI exists to maintain the framework, not replace it. Intelligence without a coherent underlying framework is just noise.

---

## The VO2 Max of the Product

What's the connective tissue between the four pillars?

**Self-regulation** — the psychological capacity to maintain goal-directed behavior under real-world conditions.

It maps to VO2 max because it's:
- Trainable
- Depletable
- Measurable in research literature
- The thing that directly explains why two people with identical plans get different outcomes

If self-regulation is the VO2 max of this product, then Goal2 isn't just helping users execute today's plan — it's gradually expanding their capacity to handle more complex goals over time.

---

## The Core Reframe

"You don't have to be very smart. You just have to do these things."

This inverts how most goal achievement discourse works. Typically the burden is on the person — be more disciplined, more motivated, more self-aware. Goal2 inverts that: the intelligence is in the plan.

Like Runna — it doesn't ask how your legs feel. It just says run 8 miles at 9:30 pace. Life happens, but the app is there to tell you what you need to do to achieve the goal.

The product assumes nothing about the user's discipline or metacognitive skill. It hands them a prescription and says follow this.

---

## Strategic Choices

**Performance product, not practice product.**
Open (breathwork app) works without clean outcome attribution because the feedback loop is minutes long — you feel calmer immediately. Education doesn't work that way. The attribution problem has to be solved, not avoided.

**No metacognitive labor required.**
The biggest vulnerability of adaptive systems is that they require disciplined engagement — logging, reflecting, adjusting — from the exact user who struggles with disciplined execution. Runna doesn't have this problem because GPS and heart rate give it data passively.

Goal2 doesn't need to adapt based on how the user feels. It just tells them what to do today. The prescription is the product.

---

## The Two Upstream Failure Modes

1. **Thinking too small** — ambition is unconsciously constrained below actual capability. Most people never attempt their real goal.

2. **Living in the immediate** — even with big goals, attention collapses to the nearest task, losing the thread between today's behavior and the long-term outcome.

Systems that force big thinking fail at daily execution. Systems that nail daily execution gradually compress ambition. Goal2 has to hold both simultaneously — taking the audacious goal as input and making today's work feel like a direct expression of it.

---

## Competitive Landscape

| App | What it does | What it misses |
|-----|-------------|----------------|
| Syllabus AI | Scans syllabus, extracts deadlines | No learning science in the prescription |
| Ahead | Schedule generation | Static, deadline-proportional |
| StudyFetch | AI study tools | Fragmented features, not a coaching system |
| OVR IT | Syllabus + task management | Task manager, not a coach |

**The gap:** None of them apply actual learning science to the daily prescription. They're all calendar tools wearing coaching clothing.
