# Goal2 — Study Coaching App

> "You don't have to be very smart. You just have to do these things."

## The Idea

Goal2 is a study coaching app that works like Runna for running. You photograph your syllabus, set your goal, and the app builds you a daily study prescription — not just a calendar, but a scientifically-grounded plan that tells you exactly what to do and when.

The market has plenty of syllabus scanners. What it's missing is coaching intelligence underneath one.

## The Problem

Most study apps divide your time evenly across deadlines. That's the academic equivalent of telling a runner to jog the same distance every day until race day.

Real coaching doesn't work that way. It accounts for difficulty, cognitive load, how memory actually works, and the gap between what you think will take an hour and what actually takes three.

Goal2 applies the same logic elite athletes use — but to studying.

## The Four Pillars

1. **Output** — controls the tangible behavior that produces results, not just the outcome
2. **Structure** — adaptive scaffolding for when and how work gets done (temporal *and* behavioral)
3. **Prioritization** — dynamically deadline-driven, difficulty-weighted, and capacity-aware
4. **Intelligence** — the system that serves the other three; not the product in itself

## What Makes the Prescription Good

- Spaced repetition logic
- Interleaving across subjects
- Retrieval practice over passive review
- Effort estimation correction
- Cognitive load awareness

## Core Philosophy

The intelligence is in the plan, not the person.

Like Runna — the app doesn't ask how you feel today. It just tells you what you need to do to hit your goal. Take the audacious goal as input, reverse-engineer every single day from it, and hand the user a prescription they can follow without needing to be especially disciplined or self-aware.

## The Two Problems We Solve

1. **Thinking too small** — people unconsciously constrain their ambition below their actual capability
2. **Living in the immediate** — even with big goals, attention collapses to the nearest task, losing the thread to the long-term outcome

Goal2 holds that thread for you.
